Redundancy

Redundancy is when an excessive number of words are used to describe something, unnecessarily lengthening the content without enhancing its quality.

By identifying and eliminating redundancy, you can ensure that your content is concise, clear, and effectively communicates your intended message. Follow the guidance below to refine your writing.

Redundant adjectives

An adjective is a word that modifies a noun or pronoun.

Andy formed a three-part love triangle with Anna Friel and Kate Winslet.

In this regrettably fictional narrative, the noun triangle already implies three parts, making the adjective three-part redundant. You can remove the adjective with no loss of meaning.

Andy formed a love triangle with Anna Friel and Kate Winslet.

Here are further examples:

WITH REDUNDANCY WITHOUT REDUNDANCY
Andy is an unmarried bachelor. Andy is a bachelor.
Arria is excited to announce this new innovation. Arria is excited to announce this innovation.
The bonus was an unexpected surprise. The bonus was a surprise.
This blog provides a brief summary of the topic. This blog provides a summary of the topic.
There are four different kinds of sentences in English. There are four kinds of sentences in English.

A bachelor is unmarried, innovation is new, and a surprise is unexpected!

KEY

Remove adjectives that are redundant due to their nouns' inherent meanings.

Redundant adverbs

An adverb modifies verbs, adjectives, other verbs, or entire sentences.

This sentence contains two adverb-related redundancies:

I am absolutely certain that we must postpone the update until a later date.

The adverb absolutely is redundant with certain, which already suggests certainty. Likewise, until a later date is unnecessary with postpone, as postponement implies a future action.

I am certain that we must postpone the update.

There remains some redundancy. The adjective certain is redundant with must as the verb already implies a strong sense of necessity. We can simplify the sentence to:

We must postpone the update.

Here are further examples:

WITH REDUNDANCY WITHOUT REDUNDANCY
Reread Section 2 again. Reread Section 2.
Planning ahead is essential. Planning is essential.
The project is finished entirely. The project is finished.
Revert the software back to its previous version. Revert the software to its previous version.
Kate screamed loudly at Andy to leave her alone. Kate screamed at Andy to leave her alone.
KEY

Remove adverbs that redundantly emphasise the meaning of other words.

Redundant abbreviation phrases

Avoid redundant phrases like PIN number, where number is already included in PIN (personal identification number). This type of redundancy becomes clear when the abbreviation is fully written out.

REDUNDANT PHRASE EXPANDED FORM
ATM machine automated teller machine machine
BASIC code Beginners' All-purpose Symbolic Instruction Code code
CAD design computer-aided design design
LAN network local area network network
LCD display liquid-crystal display display
PDF format Portable Display Format format
RAM memory random-access memory memory
SCSI interface Small Computer System Interface interface
KEY

Ensure that the word after an abbreviation isn't already part of the abbreviation.

Clunky phrases to avoid

Using these clunkers is unforgiveable and punishable by hanging.

A total of

You can often remove this phrase with no loss of meaning.

NOT RECOMMENDED The analysis detected a total of 13 anomalies.
RECOMMENDED The analysis detected 13 anomalies.

Top

Due to the fact that

Replace this clunky phrase with because.

NOT RECOMMENDED You can't publish due to the fact that your script contains errors.
RECOMMENDED You can't publish because your script contains errors.

Top

During the course of

This phrase is needlessly long — just use during.

<
NOT RECOMMENDED Orders correlated strongly with Profit during the course of Q1.
RECOMMENDED Orders correlated strongly with Profit during Q1.

First and foremost

The word first suffices.

NOT RECOMMENDED First and foremost, organise your scripts.
RECOMMENDED First, organise your scripts.

Top

In actual fact

Replace this phrase with actually or omit it entirely.

NOT RECOMMENDED In actual fact, neither claim is true.
RECOMMENDED Actually, neither claim is true.

Top

In order to

The word to suffices.

NOT RECOMMENDED Switch to Code View in order to see the underlying HTML.
RECOMMENDED Switch to Code View to see the underlying HTML.

Top

In terms of

When possible, replace this phrase with a single preposition.

NOT RECOMMENDED The result is expressed in terms of a percentage.
RECOMMENDED The result is expressed as a percentage.

Top

In the process of

When possible, omit this phrase.

NOT RECOMMENDED This tool helps when you're in the process of debugging scripts.
RECOMMENDED This tool helps when you're debugging scripts.

Top

Needless to say

This phrase is needless — kill it with extreme prejudice!

NOT RECOMMENDED Needless to say, the potential is enormous.
RECOMMENDED The potential is enormous.

Top

Whether or not

The word whether suffices.

NOT RECOMMENDED This will depend on whether or not he's suitable for the job.
RECOMMENDED This will depend on whether he's suitable for the job.

Top

With the exception of

Replace this phrase with except.

NOT RECOMMENDED Net Profit increased in all countries with the exception of France.
RECOMMENDED Net Profit increased in all countries except France.

Top

At the end of the day

Replace this phrase with ultimately or omit it entirely.

NOT RECOMMENDED At the end of the day, the decision is yours.
RECOMMENDED Ultimately, the decision is yours.
